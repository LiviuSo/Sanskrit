### **Sanskrit Verse (IAST)**

**tasmāt tvam indriyāṇy ādau niyamya bharatarṣabha |**

**pāpmānaṁ prajahihy enaṁ jñāna-vijñāna-nāśanam || 3.41 ||**

---

### **Word-by-Word Translation with Grammatical Breakdown**

| **Sanskrit Word**               | **Grammar & Meaning**                                                      |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **tasmāt**                     | *adverb*– "therefore, hence"                                                  |
| **tvam**                        | *pronoun, nominative singular*– "you"                                         |
| **indriyāṇi**                 | *noun, accusative plural*– "the senses"                                       |
| **ādau**                       | *adverb*– "at first, in the beginning"                                        |
| **niyamya**                     | *gerund*– "having controlled"                                                 |
| **bharatarṣabha**              | *noun, vocative singular*– "O best of the Bharatas (Arjuna)"                  |
| **pāpmānam**                  | *noun, accusative singular*– "the sinful one (desire/lust)"                   |
| **prajahi**                     | *verb, imperative, 2nd person singular*– "completely give up, abandon"        |
| **enam**                        | *pronoun, accusative singular*– "this (desire/lust)"                          |
| **jñāna-vijñāna-nāśanam** | *compound noun, accusative singular*– "the destroyer of knowledge and wisdom" |

---

### **Reconstructed Word Order (Prose Form in Sanskrit)**

**tasmāt tvam ādau indriyāṇi niyamya,**

**bharatarṣabha, enam pāpmānam prajahi,**

**(jahihi) jñāna-vijñāna-nāśanam.**

---

### **Structured Translation (Following the Sanskrit Syntax Closely)**

* **"Therefore" (tasmāt), "you" (tvam),**

  → **"having controlled" (niyamya) "the senses" (indriyāṇi) "at first" (ādau),**

  → **"O best of the Bharatas" (bharatarṣabha),**

  → **"abandon" (prajahi) "this" (enam) "sinful one" (pāpmānam),**

  → **"(which is) the destroyer of" (nāśanam) "knowledge and wisdom" (jñāna-vijñāna).**

---

### **Final Smooth Translation**

"Therefore, O best of the Bharatas (Arjuna), having first controlled the senses, abandon this sinful (lust), which is the destroyer of knowledge and wisdom."
